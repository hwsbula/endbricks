# Secondary CTA Asset Brief — Downloadable Framework / Checklist

**Project:** Endbricks — AI Implementation Readiness Checklist
**Asset type:** Gated downloadable PDF (lead generation)
**Strategic purpose:** Secondary conversion path for buyers who are not ready to book a consultation

---

## Context

Endbricks is an AI integration consultancy positioned around operational results over hype. The website currently has one conversion point: "Book a Consultation." This is a high-commitment ask that does not serve buyers who are in research mode or building internal consensus.

This checklist is the solution. It is a free downloadable resource that captures buyer intent earlier in the decision cycle, demonstrates Endbricks' expertise in a tangible form, and creates a lead nurture path into the consultation.

The checklist must do two things equally well:
1. Give the buyer something genuinely useful — not a marketing brochure disguised as a tool
2. Surface the exact problems Endbricks solves, so the consultation feels like the natural next step

---

## Who to Hire

### Primary hire — Content Strategist / B2B Copywriter

Look for someone with experience writing lead generation assets (frameworks, checklists, guides) for B2B professional services or consulting firms. This is not a generalist content writer role. The right person understands:

- B2B buying psychology and long sales cycles
- How to structure a checklist so it is diagnostic, not decorative
- How to write copy that builds authority without overselling
- The difference between a checklist that gets downloaded and forgotten and one that drives follow-on action

**Where to find them:** Toptal, Contra, or a targeted LinkedIn search for "B2B content strategist" or "SaaS / professional services copywriter." Look for portfolio samples that include frameworks, lead magnets, or downloadable assets — not just blog posts.

### Secondary hire — Designer (PDF / Print Design)

The checklist needs to be visually credible. A well-designed PDF signals quality and professionalism before the buyer reads a single word. Look for a designer who can work within an established brand system (Endbricks uses Manrope as the primary typeface, a dark/neutral color palette, and a clean, minimal aesthetic).

**Scope:** Single designer engagement to produce the final PDF layout once copy is finalized. This can be sourced on Dribbble, Contra, or 99designs. Expect 1–3 revision rounds.

**Note:** Do not start design until copy is approved. Design built around weak copy produces a polished asset that still does not convert.

## Deliverable

A gated, downloadable PDF asset. Working title:

> **"The AI Implementation Readiness Checklist — What to Assess Before You Build, Automate, or Scale"**

(Title is directional — the copywriter should refine it.)

**Format:**
- 2–4 pages. Concise is correct. This is not a guide or a whitepaper — it is a structured decision tool.
- Designed for readability on screen and in print.
- Includes an Endbricks brand mark, a brief one-line descriptor of the firm, and a clear CTA at the end pointing to the consultation booking page.

**Tone:**
- Consistent with the Endbricks brand: measured, direct, operational. No hype, no buzzwords, no inflated claims.
- Written from the perspective of an experienced practitioner, not a vendor.
- The buyer should feel like they are getting access to the internal thinking of someone who has seen AI implementations succeed and fail.

## Strategic Framing for the Copywriter

The checklist should be organized around the decisions and conditions that determine whether an AI implementation succeeds or stalls. It is not a list of AI tools to evaluate — it is an operational readiness assessment.

### Suggested Structure (guide, not prescriptive)

**Section 1 — Process Readiness**
Is the workflow stable and documented enough to automate? Buyers often try to automate broken processes. This section helps them identify whether process cleanup needs to come first.

**Section 2 — Use Case Clarity**
Is the use case specific enough to scope and measure? Many pilots fail because the problem statement is too vague. This section helps buyers define what they are actually trying to solve.

**Section 3 — Data and Systems**
Do the right inputs exist in an accessible form? AI implementations depend on data quality and system access. This section surfaces integration and data readiness gaps early.

**Section 4 — Stakeholder and Change Readiness**
Is there internal alignment on ownership, change management, and adoption? Implementation failure is often organizational, not technical. This section prompts buyers to assess their own readiness to manage change.

**Section 5 — Success Criteria**
Can the outcome be measured? If a buyer cannot define what success looks like, they cannot evaluate whether the implementation worked. This section prompts them to define metrics before they build.

### Checklist Item Design Principles

Each item should:
- Be written as a yes/no or done/not-done question, not a generic prompt
- Be specific enough that a "no" answer clearly signals a risk or a gap
- Be written at the operational level, not the strategic level — buyers should be able to answer these questions based on real knowledge of their own workflows

**Example of a weak item:** *"Do you have a clear AI strategy?"*
**Example of a strong item:** *"Can you name the specific workflow step where time is lost or errors occur most frequently?"*

The difference is diagnostic specificity. Strong items reveal something to the buyer. Weak items prompt them to nod and move on.

## Integration with the Website

Once the asset exists, it needs to be integrated into the site in three places:

1. **Homepage hero** — alongside "Book a Consultation," add a secondary CTA: *"Not ready to book? Start here."* linking to a simple email capture page
2. **Case study pages** — at the bottom of each case study, after the result: *"Wondering if your workflow is ready for this? Download the checklist."*
3. **Contact page sidebar** — as an alternative path for visitors who land on the contact page but hesitate to submit the form

The email capture mechanism (form, delivery, and follow-up sequence) is a separate implementation task and is not in scope for the copywriter or designer. That work sits with whoever manages the site and email infrastructure.

---

## What Success Looks Like

The asset is working when:
- Buyers download it before booking a consultation and reference it in the conversation
- The consultation conversations start at a more specific, operational level than cold inquiries
- The email list built from downloads converts at a meaningfully higher rate than cold traffic

The asset is not working if:
- It reads like marketing material rather than a practitioner tool
- Buyers download it and do not engage further
- The checklist items are too generic to prompt real self-assessment

1. This brief
2. The AUDIT.md file (it contains the positioning, tone guidance, and the strategic rationale behind this asset)
